About


Currently, I am in the joint training program between Northeastern University School of Medicine and the University of Dundee in the UK, and I am a student majoring in Biomedical Engineering. Biomedical engineering is a highly cutting-edge and interdisciplinary field that integrates knowledge systems from multiple disciplines such as medicine, biology, and engineering. It is committed to tackling complex challenges in the healthcare field and contributing to the cause of human health. My research focuses on medical signal and image analysis, and utilizes artificial intelligence algorithms to carry out computer-aided diagnosis and treatment decision-making related work. The research projects I have participated in mainly cover two directions. One is the construction of an electrocardiogram signal classification model and the deployment of embedded devices. At present, I am committed to building an open source sharing platform aimed at unifying evaluation standards, building the industry's largest benchmark, and promoting the standardization and normalization of the field of electrocardiogram signal analysis. Secondly, it is to explore the application of large-scale models such as SAM and CLIP in the field of medical imaging, including image segmentation and cross disciplinary research with genomics. By combining advanced artificial intelligence technology with medical image analysis, we expect to make breakthrough progress in precision medicine and personalized treatment, providing stronger technical support for medical diagnosis and treatment.
